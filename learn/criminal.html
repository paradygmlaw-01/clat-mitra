Criminal Law ke core ideas.

Criminal Law kya hai?

Criminal Law society ke against kiye gaye wrong ko define aur punish karti hai. Yeh State vs Person hota hai. India mein ab yeh BNS 2023 ke under aata hai (pehle IPC 1860).

Har Principle ko IF-THEN-UNLESS mein todo.

IPC = old lawBNS = current (July 2024+)

Principle

"An act does not make a person guilty unless the mind is also guilty."

Actus non facit reum nisi mens sit rea

Hindi mein

Crime ke liye Actus Reus + Mens Rea dono zaruri. Sirf act karne se crime nahi banta.

Example

Facts: Arjun ne socha yeh uska umbrella hai — utha liya. Asal mein Vijay ka tha.
Answer: Theft nahi — Mens Rea absent. Genuine mistake of fact.

⚠ CLAT Trap

"Kaam hua = crime" — WRONG. Mens Rea check karo.

IF-THEN-UNLESS

IF

Actus Reus + Mens Rea dono

THEN

Criminal Liability

UNLESS

Valid Defence lagu

IPC 96-97BNS 34-35

Principle

"Every person has a right to defend their body or property against an unlawful act, provided the force used is not more than necessary."

Hindi mein

Self-defence valid hai — lekin force proportionate honi chahiye. Excess force = defence right khatam.

Example

Facts: Sanjay par Raja ne mutthi se hamla kiya. Sanjay ne knife se vaar kiya.
Answer: Excess defence — proportionate nahi. Sanjay liable (IPC 97 / BNS 35).

⚠ CLAT Trap

"Self-defence mein kuch bhi kiya = automatically free" — WRONG. Proportionate hona zaruri.

IF-THEN-UNLESS

IF

Unlawful attack + proportionate response

THEN

Not guilty

UNLESS

Excessive force

IPC 84BNS 22

Principle

"Nothing is an offence done by a person who, at the time of doing it, by reason of mental illness, is incapable of knowing the nature of the act or that it is wrong."

IPC 84 vs BNS 22

BNS 22 mein "unsoundness of mind" ki jagah "mental illness" word use hua. Law same, language updated.

Hindi mein

Act ke waqt agar person mental illness ke kaaran right/wrong nahi jaanta tha — to crime nahi. Mens Rea absent.

Example

Facts: Ravi mental illness ke kaaran nahi jaanta tha ki voh kisi ko maar raha hai.
Answer: Not guilty (IPC 84 / BNS 22) — Insanity defence.

⚠ CLAT Trap

"Mental illness hai = automatically defence" — WRONG. Us exact moment right/wrong ki knowledge absent honi chahiye (Legal Insanity ≠ Medical Insanity).

IPC 107 = BNS 45IPC 108 = BNS 46

Principle

"A person abets the doing of a thing who instigates, conspires with, or intentionally aids another person to commit an offence."

Hindi mein

Jo kisi ko crime karne ke liye uksata hai, saazish rachta hai, ya jaanboojhkar madad karta hai — voh Abettor hai. Equally liable.

Example

Facts: Priya ne Deepak ko bataya ki shaam ko Suresh akela hoga. Deepak ne robbery ki.
Answer: Priya — Abetment (IPC 107 / BNS 45). Equally liable.

⚠ CLAT Trap

"Maine khud crime nahi kiya" — defence nahi. Abettor equally liable hai.

Abetment ke 3 Tarike

1. Instigation — uksaana

2. Conspiracy — saazish rachna

3. Aid — jaanboojhkar madad karna

IPC 511BNS 62

Principle

"Whoever attempts to commit an offence and does any act towards its commission shall be punishable, even if the offence is not completed."

BNS 62 mein badlaav

BNS 62 mein "direct action toward offence" ki language clarify hui. Punishment: max sentence ki aadhi tak.

Hindi mein

Crime complete na ho — attempt bhi punishable hai. Intention + koi action zaruri.

Example

Facts: Karan ne bank lootne ki koshish ki — alarm baja, bhaag gaya.
Answer: Attempt (IPC 511 / BNS 62) — Karan liable.

⚠ CLAT Trap

"Crime poora nahi hua = no punishment" — WRONG. Attempt punishable hai.

Option choose karo — turant feedback milega.

Q1 · Mens Rea Aasan

Principle:

An act does not make a person guilty unless the mind is also guilty. Genuine mistake of fact removes mens rea.

Facts:

Meena ne ek jacket uthayi — usne socha yeh uski purani jacket hai. Asal mein voh Rekha ki thi.

Kya Meena theft ki doshi hai?

Correct: (C)

Genuine mistake of fact = Mens Rea absent = No crime. IPC 76 / BNS 14 ke under protected.

Baaki galat:

(A): Physical act alone = crime nahi. Guilty mind zaruri.

(B): Theft mein dishonest intention zaruri hai.

(D): Reason galat hai — asal kaaran absent mens rea hai.

Q2 · Self-Defence
IPC 96-97BNS 34-35

Principle:

Every person has a right to defend themselves, provided the force used is not more than necessary. (IPC 96-97 / BNS 34-35)

Facts:

Ajay ne Bunty ko ek thappad maara. Bunty ne gusse mein Ajay ko patthar se sir par maara — Ajay seriously injured ho gaya.

Kya Bunty self-defence mein tha?

Correct: (B)

Thappad ka jawab patthar se = disproportionate. IPC 96/BNS 34: force "not more than necessary." Defence exceed hui.

Baaki galat:

(A): Pehla attack = right tha, lekin proportionate response tak hi.

(C): Right tha — lekin exceed kiya.

(D): Right tha — reason galat hai.

Q3 · Abetment
IPC 107BNS 45

Principle:

Whoever intentionally aids another person to commit an offence is said to abet that offence and is equally liable. (IPC 107 / BNS 45)

Facts:

Nisha jaanti thi ki Rahul uske neighbour ke ghar mein chori karna chahta hai. Nisha ne Rahul ko neighbour ki chutti ki date aur ghar ki layout bata di. Rahul ne chori ki.

Kya Nisha liable hai?

Correct: (C)

Intentional aid = Abetment. IPC 107 / BNS 45: Equally liable. Abettor ko khud crime nahi karna padta.

Baaki galat:

(A): IPC 108/BNS 46 — Abettor equally liable.

(B): Information dena = intentional aid = abetment.

(D): Rahul ka free decision abetment nahi hatata.

Criminal Law ke CLAT-specific tips.

💡 Tip 1 — Hamesha Mens Rea check karo

Crime = Actus Reus + Mens Rea. Sirf act hua ≠ guilty.

💡 Tip 2 — Mistake of Fact vs Law

Fact mistake = valid defence (IPC 76/BNS 14). Law mistake = no excuse.

💡 Tip 3 — Self-Defence: Proportionate Force

Excess force = IPC 96-97/BNS 34-35 ka right khatam. CLAT mein yeh trap bahut aata hai.

💡 Tip 4 — Abettor equally liable

IPC 107-108 / BNS 45-46. "Maine nahi kiya" — defence nahi.

💡 Tip 5 — Attempt bhi Punishable

IPC 511 / BNS 62. Crime complete na ho — attempt punishable.

💡 Tip 6 — IPC 84 / BNS 22 Insanity

Medical illness ≠ Legal insanity. Us exact moment incapable hona zaruri.
